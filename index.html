GeoProducts falls within GeoDiscovery and builds on its principles in providing value for Canadians and the Government of Canada.

The portfolio highlights selected projects recently developed and supported by GeoProducts. Each example highlights the analytics, applications, cartography and design used to create products that meet a wide variety of needs, from responding to government priorities to sharing geographic knowledge with Canadians.

Responding to Priorities

Emergency Flood Mapping Support

Natural Resources Canada ● Emergency Geomatics Service

During the 2019 ﬂood season, Natural Resources Canada’s Emergency Geomatics Service relied on GeoProducts team to assist with ﬂood response in Eastern Canada. Hundreds of satellite images over Manitoba, Ontario, Quebec and New Brunswick were acquired, processed, and developed into unique products to support situational awareness and response planning. Results were a near-real time overview of the ﬂood situation, and critical information to emergency responders during the response phase of the emergency management cycle.

Wildland Fire Awareness Maps

Natural Resources Canada ● Departmental Emegency Operations Center

During the 2021 wildﬁre season, GeoProducts was requested by the Departmental Emergency Operations Centre (DEOC) to create wildﬁre awareness static maps for Assistant Deputy Minister (ADM) and Deputy Minister (DM) situation reports. The maps highlight active ﬁres that may impact Natural Resources Canada’s assets, critical infrastructure and First Nation communities. They were created for provinces requested by DEOC and provided twice a week for ongoing support.

Open Science Data Platform Curated Maps

Natural Resources Canada ● Strategic Policy and Innovation Sector

GeoProducts is responsible for the creation and maintenance of the dynamic maps that leverage GeoDiscovery viewer on the Open Science Data Platform for Cumulative Effects (OSDP). This includes 2 maps enabling geospatial data search within the catalogue and 8 maps based on the cumulative effects theme. GeoProducts also provides advice to the OSDP group on how geospatial data can be optimally presented within maps and on the OSDP catalogue.

Creating Knowledge

Land Cover of Canada 2015

Natural Resources Canada ● Canada Centre for Remote Sensing

Using 2015 land cover data originally derived from remotely sensed imagery by the Canadian Centre for Remote Sensing at a resolution of 30 meters, the GeoProducts team updated three products: data processing for proper visualization within the web service; interactive web mapping application; digital and print map poster. The data has been presented in an easily comprehensible way, explaining and depicting each of the land cover types found on the map.

The World

Natural Resources Canada ● The Atlas of Canada

The World map is a general reference political map focused on the names and international boundaries of sovereign and non-sovereign countries. It is a new cartographic direction for the Atlas of Canada, providing an updated map to build geographic knowledge and utilising colour palettes and typographic choices to create a fresh, new and flexible cartographic design. The Winkel II map projection preserves contiguous continents while creating a formal symmetry for the map as a whole.

Photovoltaic Potential and Solar Resource Maps of Canada

Natural Resources Canada ● CanmetENERGY

Leveraging open data from across government, GeoProducts collaborated with Natural Resources Canada’s CanmetENERGY Photovoltaic Systems group to develop a web application that comprehensively maps the photovoltaic potential of 3500 Canadian municipalities. Using data that spans a twenty-year period, the application analyzes and maps kilowatt per hour estimates of the solar power potential to generate electricity by grid-connected photovoltaic systems, by month and by year, and for six different array orientations for any location in Canada.

State of Mass Timber in Canada

Natural Resources Canada ● Canadian Forest Service

The State of Mass Timber in Canada map, database, and dashboard provide location and information of completed, under construction, or planned mass timber projects and manufacturing facilities in Canada. It was released along with the Canadian Forest Service’s 2021 SMTC report documenting the domestic mass timber construction projects and production capacity, analyzing the increase in mass timber construction and demonstrating the value for builders and designers to build with wood.

Informing Decisions

HOT2000 Climate Map

Natural Resources Canada ● Office of Energy Efficiency

The Housing Division of the Office of Energy Efficiency at Natural Resources Canada manages energy efficiency initiatives for new and existing houses. One of the key aspects is the energy analysis program, HOT2000. GeoProducts delivered key functionality and renewed geographic data to the HOT2000 suite with the development of an online web-based mapping application that delivers accurate climate data to all users, including regulatory agencies, builders, and government, exactly when and where they need it.

Major Projects Inventory

Natural Resources Canada ● Major Projects Management Office

Each year, the Economic Analysis, Data and Research Branch of the Strategic Policy and Innovation Sector collaborates with experts and partners to update the Major Projects Inventory, which tracks major projects in the energy, mining, and forest sectors that are either already under construction or planned to come under construction in the next 10 years. One national map and 13 provincial/territorial maps, in French and English, are created showing the location, type, and value of the major projects.
